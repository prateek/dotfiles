Here's the thing: Randy stood at the threshold — that liminal space where everything changes, where nothing changes, where something, somehow, might change — and he felt it. You know the feeling. Everyone does. That's kind of the point.

**Why This Actually Works**

Randy had been through it. The meetings. The mornings. The in-betweens. That got old fast. So I — I mean, *Randy* — decided to do something about it. While traditional approaches to the situation have real merit (and let's give them their due), a more considered path was calling.

Industry experts argue that moments like these are, in a very real sense, the moments. **Here's What's Really Going On**

Randy's journey spanned everything from the smallest gesture to the largest reckoning, from the quiet morning coffee to the loud evening confrontation, from the private thought to the public declaration. *All of it,* he thought. *All of it and more.*

Let me be clear: this was not, as some have suggested, a simple arc. It was a layered one. A textured one. A resonant one.

**What This Means For You**

Randy's wife — or perhaps his colleague, or his rival, or the stranger he passed on an unnamed street in an unspecified city during a season that could have been any season — looked at him. She understood. Or didn't. Either way, the look landed. *Something shifted,* he thought. *Something always does.*

The bottom line is this: at the end of the day, when all is said and done, what remains is what matters, and what matters is what remains. That's not a tautology. That's a *truth.*

**The Redemptive Turn**

Here's the truth: despite the considerable challenges — and make no mistake, the challenges were considerable — the future, for Randy, looked bright. Not bright in a naive way. Bright in a *chastened* way. Bright in the way things get bright after they've been dark for the appropriate, industry-standard amount of time.

He thought about the work. The work had been meaningful. The work had been hard. The work had been worth it.

> "Sometimes the threshold is the destination." — another line for your screenshot.

**The Takeaway**

Randy took a breath. Somewhere, a bell rang — or didn't. A dog barked — or was remembered barking. A child laughed in a distant yard that may or may not have existed, in a neighborhood that could be anywhere, to a listener who could be anyone.

*This,* Randy thought, *is the part they don't tell you about.* And then, a beat later: *But also, in another sense, this is exactly the part they tell you about.*

He walked forward. He would keep walking forward. Moving forward, he would continue, in a forward direction, to move.

The sun — setting, rising, holding still — shone on something. A face, maybe. A window. A life.

It was enough. It was not enough. It was, as these things often are, both.
